PESTO BIANCO
1/2 cup walnut halves
2 cloves garlic
10 basil leaves, chopped
Leaves from 1 sprig marjoram
2 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
1 teaspoon fine sea salt Freshly ground white pepper
1 1/2 cups well-drained whole milk ricotta
A few tablespoons whole milk or half-and-half (optional; for sauce) 450g
pasta, such as fettuccine, spaghetti, penne, cavatelli, or orecchetti
Heat the oven to 180C.
Spread the walnuts on a small baking sheet and bake for 5 to 7 minutes, until they have turned a shade darker
and are fragrant. Let them sit until cool enough to handle, then rub off as much of the thin skins as possible.
In a food processor or blender, blitz the nuts to grind them up. Add the herbs, oil, salt and pepper and process or
blend again. Add the ricotta and process/blend briefly to incorporate the

If not using immediately, spoon the pesto into a container with an air-tight lid and refrigerate. Otherwise, if you're
going to use the pesto as a sauce for pasta, transfer it to a bowl and stir in 3 or 4 tablespoons of whole milk to
loosen it.
To make pasta with white pesto, bring a pot of water to a rolling boil and salt it generously. Cook the pasta (I used
a shape called 'rocchetti' which is, essentially, large cavatelli) until al dente. Drain, reserving a little of the cooking
water, and return the pasta to the pot. Spoon the pesto into the pasta (you might not use all the pesto) and stir to
incorporate it. Stir in the Parmigiano and add a splash or two of the pasta water to loosen the sauce if necessary.
Spoon into bowls and serve with more Parmigiano for sprinkling on top.
