Sesame Udon noodles
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger root
1/4 cup soy sauce
3 tablespoons rice vinegar
1/4 cup peanut oil
3 tablespoons sesame oil
1 dash hot pepper sauce
1/2 green bell pepper, julienned
1/2 red bell pepper, julienned
1/2 yellow bell pepper, julienned
4 green onions, minced
2 cups diagonally sliced snap peas
2 tablespoons sesame seeds, toasted
1 (7 ounce) package fresh udon noodles
In a bowl combine the garlic, ginger, soy sauce, rice vinegar, peanut oil, sesame oil and hot pepper sauce. Whisk
well until well blended. Set aside to let the flavors blend.
Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add udon noodles, and cook until tender, about 3 minutes. Drain, and place
in a serving bowl.
In a wok or heavbassed frying pan, add a little oil, and then saute the green, red, and yellow peppers with green
onion and peas. Fry until they being to collapse. Add to the noodles to the pan and toss to coat.
Now pour the dressing over all. Toss to coat everything in dressing, remove from the heat, and then sprinkle
toasted sesame seeds over the top.
